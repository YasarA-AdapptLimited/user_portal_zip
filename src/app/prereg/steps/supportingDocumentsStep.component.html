Supporting documents

Please upload scanned copies of the documents we need to verify the information you have given us as part of your application to register as pharmacist. Click on the ‘I’ icon next to each section to find out more about the documents you need to provide. Read the application guidance on providing correctly certified documents to check how to get copies of your original documents certified correctly.

Important: the files you upload here must be scanned versions of the certified copies of your documents. Make sure your scanned versions are clear and the text is readable. You must post hard copies of the documents you have scanned to us, with an application cover sheet.

We will never ask you for the original versions of your identity and qualification documents and you should not send these to us.

You will also need to complete and upload a statutory declaration form if:

  • If your birth certificate is not written in English or the registration date on your birth certificate is not within one year of your birth
  • your name is written differently on any of your supporting documents
  • you want to register using a different version of your name than the one on your birth certificate

See the application guidance for more information about completing a statutory declaration form.

Upload a scanned version of your foundation training final declaration which has been signed off by your designated supervisor.

Upload a scanned version of your certified copy of your proof of identity document(s)

Important: All documents you submit as proofs of your identity must be correctly certified copies of original documents which are valid on the date you submit your application.

What documents can be used as proof of identity ?

As proof of identity, you can submit a correctly certified copy of either:

  • a page of your current valid passport page showing your photo and the expiry date
  • or, if you do not have a current valid passport, you can submit a correctly certified copy of:
  • your valid full or provisional photocard driving licence together with a correctly certified copy of your:
  • UK national Insurance card
  • NHS card
  • photo identity card from an non-UK EEA country

Your name should be written exactly the same on all the documents you submit as proof of identity. If any letter or word in your name is written differently on any of them, you must complete a statutory declaration form – see the application guidance for more information.

Upload a scan of your birth certificate. If you were born in England, Wales and Scotland, please upload your birth certificate issued by the General Registry Office, or the General Registers Office for Scotland.

If you were NOT born in England, Wales or Scotland you must submit a certified copy of your birth certificate. Any document that is not written in English must be accompanied by a translation.

If you are not able to provide a birth certificate you can submit Statutory Declaration A, which needs to be completed with a solicitor.

Upload a scanned version of your MPharm or OSPAP qualification certificate. The name on the certificate must match the one on your identification document. If it does not, see the application guidance and complete a statutory declaration form.

Upload a recent photograph of yourself and a completed photo certification form that has been countersigned by one of the people who is listed as an acceptable counter signatory by the Home Office passport service. This should be someone who has known you for at least two years and is resident in the UK.

If you need to submit any other documents such as a statutory declaration form or proof of change of name, upload them here.
