Notice of removal from the GPhC register for revalidation non-compliance

On {{ letter?.data.NIRLetterSentDate | date: 'd MMMM yyyy' }}, we issued you with a notice of intention to remove your entry from the register. The notice explained that not submitting a complete revalidation record and not providing evidence of exceptional circumstances would mean that your registration is at risk. The notice also set out the options available to you, with a response date of {{ letter?.data.NIRResponseDate | date: 'd MMMM yyyy' }}.

To date we have not received a response from you. You have not submitted your complete revalidation record or provided a written representation, explaining why you have been unable to submit your revalidation record or why your entry should not be removed from the register.

I am writing to inform you that your entry will be removed from the register on {{ letter?.data.RemovalDate | date: 'd MMMM yyyy' }} for failure to comply with the revalidation framework. This means that you will no longer be able to practise as a {{ registrantType }} from this date.

Under article 40 of the Pharmacy Order 2010, you have a right to appeal the decision to remove your entry from the GPhC register. If you want to do this, you must submit a completed ‘notice of appeal’ form by {{appealDate | date: 'd MMMM yyyy'}}.

The form and guidance about how to complete it is available from the registration section of the GPhC website at www.pharmacyregulation.org/decisions-about-your-application-or-registration

The process followed by the appeals committee is set out in the GPhC (Appeals Committee Rules) Order of Council 2010. The appeals committee has the power to award costs against any party to an appeal, so you may want to seek independent legal advice before lodging an appeal.

Important: If you submit a notice of appeal in relation your removal, we may suspend your entry from the register until the final outcome of your appeal. This is set out in the GPhC (Continuing Professional Development and Consequential Amendments Rules) Order of Council 2011. Practising as a pharmacist or pharmacy technician while not on the register is a criminal offence. Make sure that you have appropriate staffing cover in place after your removal date if necessary, and that you notify your employer of your removal date, if you are not self-employed. You should also check your indemnity arrangements, as these may need to continue after your removal.

You can apply to restore your entry to the register by completing an application for restoration, which you can find on our website. Please note you must submit the outstanding revalidation records with your application for restoration.

If you have any queries or require any further information, please contact us by email at info@pharmacyregulation.org or on 020 3713 8000.
