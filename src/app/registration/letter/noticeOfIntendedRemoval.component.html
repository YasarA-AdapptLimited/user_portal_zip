Notice of intended removal from the GPhC register

I am writing to confirm that your myGPhC record shows you have failed to submit your complete revalidation record to the GPhC. Please be aware that this notice is not related to the renewal of your registration.

We have previously reminded you that your registration is at risk if you do not submit a complete revalidation record, and do not provide evidence of exceptional circumstances which explain why you have not submitted a record.

Under the GPhC (Continuing Professional Development and Consequential Amendments Rules) Order of Council 2011, we may take action that could lead to your removal from the register.

If you want to stay on the register and continue to practise as a pharmacy professional, you have two options at this stage. You must either:

  1. submit your complete revalidation record via myGPhc, or
  2. write to us, setting out the reasons why your entry should not be removed from the register. You must include any relevant evidence of exceptional circumstances in support of your reasons. Address your written reasons to the registrar.

We must receive your complete revalidation record, or your written reasons with supportive evidence, within 28 days of the date of this letter. If we do not, we may remove you from the register. Please note that this process is separate to the renewal of your registration. If you have not yet renewed your registration you may still be removed from the register for failure to renew, even if you now submit your revalidation record.

If you choose to send us your written reasons and supportive evidence explaining why you were unable to submit your revalidation record, this information will be considered by the registrar, who will then decide whether or not you may continue to be registered.

You also have the option of requesting that your reasons and evidence be considered by the Fitness to Practise Committee at a hearing. If you would prefer that a hearing takes place, you must notify the GPhC when you send in your written reasons. If you do not state clearly that you want a hearing to take place, the registrar will make the decision about your registration without a hearing taking place.

Important: if you do not submit your written reasons and any relevant supportive evidence, you do not have the option of requesting a hearing. The registrar will make the decision about whether or not you may continue to be registered.

If you request a hearing, the purpose of the hearing will be for the Fitness to Practise Committee to make ‘findings of fact’. The registrar will use these findings in making the final decision regarding your registration.

For more information about what will happen at a hearing, see Rule 33 of The GPhC (Fitness to Practise and Disqualification etc. Rule) Order of Council 2010.

This is your final reminder to take action. Your register entry may be removed if you do not respond to this request.

If you have any questions, please contact our Customer Services Team by email at info@pharmacyregulation.org
