This requirement is set out in the Pharmacy Order 2010.

To renew your registration, you must log on to www.myGPhC.org and:

  • complete a set of declarations to confirm that you are fit to practise as a pharmacy professional
  • pay a registration renewal fee of {{letter?.data.AnnualRenewalAmount | currency: 'GBP' : 'symbol' : '.2'}}.
  • submit your revalidation records covering your previous 12-month period of registration

Ways to pay

We recommend that you pay your renewal fee by debit card, as you can do this easily when you log on to myGPhC to make your declarations. You can also pay by Direct Debit, either once a year, or divide your renewal fee into four quarterly payments. If you want to pay by Direct Debit, you will need to set this up by {{ letter?.data.DDMandateDeadline | date:'d MMMM yyyy' }}. See www.pharmacyregulation.org/renew for more information on setting up a Direct Debit.

If you don’t renew your registration by the date above, we will take action to remove your entry from the register when your registration expires. You are not able to practise as a {{registrantType}} unless you are registered.

Meeting the revalidation requirement

You can find more about what you need to record and submit in the revalidation framework. The framework and other supporting materials are available on our website at www.pharmacyregulation.org/revalidation

If you do not want to renew your registration

If you’re not intending to renew your registration, tell us that you no longer want to be registered by completing an Application for voluntary removal form, available on our website. This helps us to keep the register information accurate and allows you to choose when you leave the register. There is no charge to leave the register.

If you need more information about renewing your registration, please go to www.pharmacyregulation.org/renew or email our Customer Contact Centre at info@pharmacyregulation.org.
