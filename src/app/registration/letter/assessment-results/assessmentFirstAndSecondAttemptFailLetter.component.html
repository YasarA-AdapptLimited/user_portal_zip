To pass the assessment, you must pass part one and part two in the same sitting. For more information on how we award marks and set pass marks for the registration assessment please go to the Assuring the assessment page on the main GPhC website.

You are permitted three attempts at the registration assessment. You must make these within the maximum time limit for completion of pharmacy education and training set out in the set out in the GPhC Criteria for registration as a pharmacist.

Until you pass the registration assessment and meet all the registration requirements you are not eligible to apply for registration as a pharmacist, and you must not work as a pharmacist or call yourself one.

If you want to re-sit the assessment, you will need to make sure that you meet the eligibility requirements to sit and submit another application.

For information about your next steps including resitting or considering an appeal, see the Guidance for unsuccessful candidates in the registration assessment, which we hope you will find helpful.
