When should I use this form ?

We want to make sure that if you are practising as a pharmacist or pharmacy technician, you are carrying out revalidation activity. But we understand that in some circumstances, you may not be practising for at least three months of your registration year. Or that you may need extra time to record or submit records of activities from a period of registration. This might be because of sick leave, maternity leave, military postings, or breaks from practice, for example.

This means that you may not be able to meet to submit some or all of your revalidation records when you renew your registration. It’s important that you tell us as soon as you know that you will not be able to do this– you can use this form at any time.

Use this form to tell us about exceptional circumstances where:

  • you have not been (or will not be) practising for more than three months of your registration year and have not been (or will not be) able to carry out all the required revalidation activity
  • you have been practising for at least nine months of the registration year but you may not be able to submit your records when you renew your registration
  • Make a request, and provide supporting evidence, to extend your submission deadline, or submit fewer entries.

Important: If you don’t think you will be able to meet the revalidation requirement even with these changes, please contact us.

We will use the information you provide to consider your request. We will contact you to let you know if we are satisfied with your reasons and the changes you have requested, or if we need to discuss the matter further. If you do not receive a response from us within 28 working days of submitting your form, please contact us.

What changes can I request?

A submission extension

You may want to request an extension if you have been practising for at least nine months of your registration year, and have carried out at least the required number of revalidation activities – but need more time to write up and submit these records. If you are practising while the extension is in place, you will still need to carry out and record new revalidation activities and submit these by your next renewal due date.

Submitting fewer records

If you have not been, or will not be, working as a pharmacy professional or carrying out revalidation activities for part of your registration year (because of a serious illness, or maternity leave, for example), you may want to request that you submit fewer revalidation records by your current renewal date. If there are any gaps in your records like this, they should not normally be longer than 12 months.

Important: If you don’t think you will be able to meet the revalidation requirement even with these changes, please contact us.

Providing supporting evidence

Please give a brief explanation of the reasons why you are aren’t able to submit all of you records by your renewal due date. Make sure you explain how your situation has affected your ability to carry out and record CPD. If you have been or are likely to be absent from work, include this too. This will help us understand the reasons for the changes you are requesting.

You must provide at least one document as evidence to support your request – we will not consider it without one. The kinds of documents you send to us will depend on the reasons for your request. For example, some of the more common reasons for requesting changes to the requirements and the kinds of documents that you could use to support these are:

  • maternity leave: a copy of your MAT1B form
  • sickness leave: a doctor’s note or other medical documents which explain your condition

Please see our privacy statement for more information on how we handle the personal information you provide to us.
