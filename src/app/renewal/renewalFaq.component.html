Renewal FAQs

Frequently asked questions regarding renewal

You need to renew your registration at least two months before it is due to expire. This requirement is to make sure the register stays up to date and is set out in our legislation.

What you need do will depend on how you pay your fee. Please see your renewal notice for more information.

We will notify you around three months before your renewal is due (five months before your registration expires) to tell you when you need to renew by – your ‘renewal due date’.

Your renewal status will also show as ‘due’ on the ‘Home’ page of your myGPhC account at the same time, and it will show your renewal due date. Click on the button to follow the renewal process.

You can renew your registration at any point once you receive your letter, or your renewal status shows as due on myGphC, until your renewal due date. See the How do I renew? section if you need help to renew your registration.

If you do not renew your registration before your renewal date and you do not want to be removed from the register, contact us.

Step 1: Decide on your payment method

You can pay your registration fee by:

  • credit card
  • debit card
  • annual or quarterly Direct Debit

Unless you have already set up a Direct Debit, you should pay by credit or debit card. If you want to pay by Direct Debit, see I want to pay by Direct Debit. How do I set one up?

Step 2: Make your declarations

  • Click on the ‘Start’ button on the ‘Renewal’ page.
  • Fill in and confirm the declarations. If you need to give more information about any of your declarations, you may asked to fill in some extra forms. Follows the prompts for more information.

Step 3: Make your payment

If you are paying by credit or debit card, please have your card with you. Once you have completed your declarations, you will be transferred to the online payment system. You may be asked by your bank to enter a password to complete your payment. You can find out more about this in What is the extra password step I encountered when making my online payment?

If you enter your email address on the payment page, you will receive a confirmation that your payment has been processed – this is not a receipt.

Once your payment is complete, you will be transferred back to MyGPhC. You have finished your renewal.

If you already have a yearly or quarterly Direct Debit set up to pay your renewal fee, all you will need to do is complete your renewal declarations by the date given in your renewal notice. Once you have done this, you will have finished your renewal.

Step 4: Collect your receipt

If you need a receipt for your renewal payment, you can view and download one from the Receipts page.

If you paid by card it may take up to 48 hours for your receipt to show.

If you are paying by Direct debit, it may take up to 21 days for your payment receipt to show.

If you are paying by quarterly Direct Debit, each time a payment is taken the amount paid and amount still owing will be updated on your receipt.

Depending on the type of card you use, you may be asked to enter a password before you can finish making your payment. This is part of 3-D Secure, a password-based security system that your bank has introduced to reduce the risks of fraudulent card use.

Different card providers use different names for this services, for example:

  • Visa: Verified by Visa
  • MasterCard: MasterCard SecureCode
  • JCB International: J/Secure

If your bank uses 3d Secure and you don’t have a password, you may be asked to set one up. If you are not sure what to do, contact your bank.

Completing your renewal using myGPhC involves submitting information which is sent as electronic data over the internet. Using your web browser's ‘Back’ button or ‘History’ feature can interrupt the sending of the information you have submitted, and some of it may be lost. If you try to use these features during your registration renewal, you will see a pop up box which will ask you to either continue or start the process again.

If you want to pay Direct Debit, you will need to set one up by the date set out in your renewal notice to make sure you can complete the process in time.

You can pay:

  • a one off annual fee
  • a portion each quarter (every three months from the date of your renewal)

We will take payments on the first working day of each month. We will send you a payment schedule to show the payments and the date(s) we will take them.

To set up a direct debit you will need to:

  1. Download a Direct Debit mandate form from the GPhC website.
  2. Fill in the form - use your registration number as the payment reference, and tick one of the boxes to show if you want to pay annually or quarterly.
  3. Send the form to us at registers@pharmacyregulation.org, or to our postal address below. The form must reach us before the date shown in Step 4 of your renewal notice.

Send your form to:

Registration Team
General Pharmaceutical Council
Level 14
One Cabot Square
London
E14 4QJ

We will send you an email notice to confirm that your Direct Debit has been set up, and a payment schedule to show the payments and the date(s) we will take them.

The notice will also give you a new deadline by which you must make your renewal declarations. If you do not complete your declarations in time, the Direct Debit will not be collected and you will need to make your full registration payment using a debit or credit card.

If you want to change from paying by direct debit to paying by card, you will need to tell us before you start your registration renewal. Allow at least six weeks to before your registration renewal is due to make sure you can make the change.

To change from paying by Direct Debit to paying by card, you must:

  1. Check your payment schedule if you pay quarterly, to see if you have any outstanding payments to make. If you do, contact us on 020 3713 8000 and we will take payment over the phone by credit or debit card. This will avoid any administration fees for incomplete payments.
  2. Contact your bank to cancel your Direct Debit.
  3. Send an email to us at registers@pharmacyregulation.org to confirm that you have cancelled your Direct Debit with your bank. Tell us your registration number so we can find your details easily and make sure that they are updated.
  4. We will send you an email giving a new date by which you need to complete your registration renewal. If you do not receive an email from us within 10 working days of your email, contact us.
  5. Sign in to myGPhC to renew your registration. After your have completed the declarations section, you should be able to pay your renewal fee by card.

If you want to change how often you make a Direct Debit payment, you need to tell us before you start your registration renewal. Please see your renewal notice to find the date by which you will need to make the change, but make sure you allow at least eight weeks to before your registration renewal is due.

To change how often you make a Direct Debit payment, you must:

  • Send an email to us at registers@pharmacyregulation.org. Tell us your registration number and how you want to change your Direct Debit (from annually to quarterly, for example).
  • We will send you an email to confirm we have made the change.
  • Complete your registration renewal declarations as usual.

If you want to change the bank details we use to collect Direct Debit payments from you, you will need to set up a new Direct Debit by completing a new Direct Debit mandate form.

If you are using the current account switching service to change accounts, we will automatically be notified of the change, so you don’t need to complete a form.

Important: We take payments on the first working day of the month they are due. Please make sure that we know about any changes at least five working days before a payment is due to be taken so that we can make sure we request the payment from the right account. If a payment is refused, you will need to pay the outstanding amount by credit or debit card.

If you don’t want or need to be registered with the GPhC any longer, you should tell us by completing a voluntary removal and we will remove your details from the register. You can access the voluntary removal form via your myGPhC account, it is located under the 'Applications' tab.

This helps us to keep the register up to date and to show your status accurately.

There is no charge to process your voluntary removal form. You can submit it any time until your registration expiry date, and you can chose when your details are removed from the register if you want.

If you complete a voluntary removal form and later want to be restored to the register, this process can be carried out more easily and with a lower fee than if you take no action and let your registration lapse.

If you have already completed you registration renewal and then decide that you want to be removed from the register, we will refund your registration fee if we receive your voluntary removal form before your registration expiry date.

If you have attempted to renew your registration after your renewal date and have not been able to, you will need to complete a restoration application.

Restoration applications can take 28 days to process, so you should fill in the application as soon as possible.
